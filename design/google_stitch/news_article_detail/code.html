Korea's Export Growth Hits Record High Amid Global AI Boom

Korea Herald

Updated 2 hours ago â€¢ 4 min read

South Korea's exports expanded at the fastest pace in over a decade this quarter, driven by an insatiable global demand for high-end semiconductors and advanced automobile components.

The Ministry of Trade, Industry and Energy reported on Sunday that outbound shipments surged by 15.4% year-on-year, marking a significant milestone in the nation's post-pandemic recovery strategy. The trade surplus has now remained in positive territory for five consecutive months, signaling a robust outlook for the second half of the year.

"We are seeing a structural shift in global demand towards AI-integrated hardware," says Park Ji-won, a senior analyst at the Korea Development Institute. "Korea's leading position in HBM (High Bandwidth Memory) chips is the primary engine behind these record-breaking figures."
